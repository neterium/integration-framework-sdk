1. Purpose and Objective
 
1.1 Purpose
 
The purpose of this open-source policy is to facilitate and accelerate the integration of the Neterium sanctions/PEP screening engine through the publication of generic software components, software development kits (SDKs), and integration examples intended to support developers in building their own applications around Neterium’s API (the “Open Source Components”, as defined in Section 2.1). This approach is designed to maximize adoption while mitigating technical, legal and business risks through a clear and consistent framework.
 
1.2 What this project is not
 
For the avoidance of doubt, the Open Source Components are not: (i) a complete end-to-end product; (ii) a full-featured user interface; or (iii) a managed service. The Open Source Components are provided as integration enablers and illustrative reference implementations, and are not intended to substitute for Neterium’s commercial offering or for solutions delivered by Neterium’s integration partners.
 
1.3 Intended audience
 
This Policy and the Open Source Components are intended for: (i) Neterium customers; (ii) Neterium integration partners and other solution providers integrating the Neterium API; and (iii) Neterium internal teams involved in product, engineering, compliance, and go-to-market activities.
 
2. Scope and Coverage
 
2.1 In-scope materials
 
This Policy applies to, and governs the publication of, the following materials made available by Neterium in the Repositories (collectively, the “Open Source Components”): (i) software development kits (SDKs) and libraries; (ii) API wrappers and helper modules; (iii) integration samples and reference implementations; (iv) a minimal user interface (UI) or demonstrator interface intended solely for illustration purposes; and (v) related documentation (including installation instructions and usage examples).
 
2.2 Excluded materials
 
For the avoidance of doubt, the Open Source Components do not include, and this Policy does not authorise or contemplate the publication of: (i) any proprietary Neterium software, including the sanctions/PEP screening engine and any non-public services, algorithms or models; (ii) data sets, sanction lists, watchlists, customer data or other data content; (iii) trade secrets, confidential information, internal tooling, credentials, keys or configuration secrets; (iv) customer-specific configurations, implementations or deliverables; or (v) any other materials that are not expressly included in Section 2.1.
 
2.3 Out of scope
 
This Policy and the Open Source Components are provided solely to facilitate integration and do not include, and shall not be construed as creating, any obligation on Neterium to provide: (i) support services, consulting, or assistance with customer integration; (ii) any service level agreement (SLA), maintenance commitment, or guaranteed updates; or (iii) hosting, operation, monitoring, or managed services in relation to the Open Source Components.
